Origin of insurance.

Insurance was known for the first time in the European countries in the late Middle Ages, and as a result of the development of trade relations between the cities of Italy and the countries of the Mediterranean basin appeared the first area of insurance, which is maritime transport.was asking arms dealers to send their weapons by sea to supply the forces of the empire, to include the value of these weapons if lost by the enemy or because of the dangers of the sea and the idea of insurance appeared more clearly in the northern cities known as Combed and evolved little by little to take the formula Collective, At the beginning of the 16th century, insurance texts were issued, such as France's completion of maritime law with an insurance book, followed by other European countries. The first insurance company was established in the maritime field in England in 1720, followed by other European countries, so insurance became an important field of commercial and economic activity. Land insurance emerged in the 17th century as the fire guard in England following the famous Benden Fire of 1966. From it, insurance entered many other fields, especially after the spread of industry and other mechanical machinery. But insurance did not appear as it is until the beginning of the current century with the issuance of a set of laws in Aruba such as Switzerland 1908 - Germany 1908 - France 1990 and prevailed in Algeria in 1980 in accordance with Law No. 80-07 of 09/08/1980.

The concept of insurance.

It is a contract that obliges the insurer to compensate the insured as a result of the risks that occur to him in exchange for the latter's payment of periodic amounts called premiums. The Algerian insurance project is also defined as "a contract under which the insurer undertakes to pay the insured income or any other financial compensation in the event of an accident or the realization of the risk set forth in the contract, in return for a premium or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer.

Types of insurance.

The diversity of insurance images dramatically until it became not confined, as insurance extends to magazines in which it did not have any role previously, and the following is a presentation of the types of insurance, which were divided into two types, the first in terms of form and the second in terms of subject. (1) I- Division of insurance in terms of form: It is intended to divide it according to the body that undertakes the insurance process, and in this regard it is also divided into two types, namely cooperative insurance (mutual) and insurance with a fixed premium.

Mutual Insurance (Cooperative):

  • In this type of insurance, a group of people associated with the link of interest and exposed to a certain risk, without the insured and the insured within the framework of a cooperative society that brings them together to compensate for the damages caused to one of them as a result of the insured risk and the amount to be compensated is paid from a set of subscriptions that each member of the association is obligated to pay and we mention the most important characteristics of this type of insurance as follows: – In cooperative insurance, the status of the insured and the insured is combined in each member of the association. To secure the cooperative is characterized by solidarity among its members.

Fixed Premiums Insurance:

  • In this type of insurance, the insurer (insurance company) is obliged to pay the insurance amount when the insured risk is realized, in return for the fixed premiums paid by the insured. Hence, the insured is not one person, and the premium committed by the insured is fixed as the insurer is committed alone before the insured, and this type of insurance is characterized by the following characteristics: – Separation of the insured from the insured. – The premium paid by the insured is fixed, that is, it is determined at a fixed amount before the conclusion of the contract. – There is no solidarity between the insured and the insured.

Partition of insurance in terms of subject matter:

    - Insurance is divided in terms of subject matter into several types, namely "marine, river, air rain, private insurance, social insurance, insurance for persons and insurance for damages"
  1. Marine, land and air insurance: This insurance is based on the nature of the risks insured against, marine insurance aims to insure the risks of maritime transport by ships, and land insurance covers the risks of land transport, while air insurance covers the risks related to air transport that aircraft may be exposed to.
  2. For private insurance and social insurance: - Private insurance means insurance carried out by a person in anticipation of a specific risk or accident expected to occur in the future, the person who performs this insurance seeks to achieve his own interest, while social insurance is a system based on achieving the public interest and is to cover the social risks to which members of the general class are exposed.
  3. Insurance against damages and insurance for persons: - This division is based on the availability or verification of the compensatory character in the insurance, if the objective of insurance is to cover the risks or financial losses caused to the financial disclosure of the insured, the insurance is insurance from damages and the insurance contract is a contract of compensation, or if the purpose of the insurance is to obtain the amount of insurance as soon as the insured is achieved and regardless of the occurrence of all or part of the damage, the insurance is insurance for persons Related to man in his life and health (1).

Insurance from damages: – In this type of insurance, the insured risk is focused on the insured's money and not his person, and this type of insurance aims to compensate the insured for the damage that may occur as a result of the occurrence of the insured risk, that is, it has the status of compensation. – This type of insurance, in turn, is divided into two main parts: insurance of things and insurance of liability:


  1. Goods insurance: This type of damage insurance aims to compensate the insured for the damages caused to him when the risk is achieved and in this way it does not contain any two parties, the insured and the insured, and insurance of things includes several types, including: Home insurance, fire, and money insurance from theft. Etcetera
  2. Liability insurance: This type aims to guarantee the insured against the return of others to him due to damages caused to them from the errors committed by the insured. In this case, insurance aims to compensate for the damage caused to third parties and cover the liability of the insured.

Personal insurance: "This insurance relates to the person of the insured and aims to pay the insurance amount if the risk occurs, and this includes insurance of risks to which a person is exposed in his life or health, as well as happy accidents such as marriage, childbearing and others. Some of the forms of insurance for persons are life insurance, accident or injury insurance and sickness insurance.The following is a breakdown of some of the types of this insurance.

  1. Life insurance: - This insurance aims to face the risk of death to which every person is exposed and at every moment, and there is no doubt that this insurance is a manifestation of foresight and precaution for the future as it is considered a credit tool from others and a means of individual savings and savings at the community level, and life insurance affects several areas, including: popular insurance, insurance for death, insurance for the state of survival, supplementary insurance... Etcetera.
  2. Insurance against accidents and injuries: In this type of insurance, the insurer is obligated (in return for a premium) to cover an accident that occurs to the insured through a sum of money that leads to the latter or to the beneficiary in the event of death.
  3. Sickness insurance: – It is a contract under which the insurer is obligated in return for periodic premiums in the event that the insured suffers from an illness during the insurance period to pay a certain amount and compensate him for the expenses of treatment and medicines, and this insurance may be insurance for the person of the insured or others, especially the family members of the insured.

State Control of Insurance Companies:

It is concerned with the control of insurance companies with regard to their activity and organs, organization, establishment and expiry, and it is a public authority with an independent legal personality and reports directly to the Minister of Economy, and the objectives of the Authority are:

  1. Protecting the rights of policyholders, beneficiaries and third parties.
  2. Ensure the achievement of the economic and social objectives of the company's insurance activity and preserve national savings and foreign exchange resources from leakage.
  3. Ensure the integrity of the financial positions of the insurance market units and coordinate and prevent conflicts between them.
  4. Participate in the development of insurance awareness in the country.
  5. Strengthening the insurance market and working to develop it.
  6. Strengthening ties of cooperation and integration with oversight and supervision bodies at the Arab, African and international levels
  7. Promotion of insurance professions and effective contribution to the provision of expertise.

Administrative Control:

The Authority reviews the insurance policies and the conditions and amendments contained thereto, and the document can only be worked after the adoption of the Authority, the Authority monitors the content of the document and its prices to prevent the exploitation of the insured and ensure equality between them, as it is not permissible to distinguish between one document and another of the documents of one type with regard to insurance prices or the amount of profits distributed to policyholders or other contributions unless this distinction is the result of different possibilities of life, and the imposition of the Algerian legislator on the insurance company To practice its activity obtain accreditation.

Technical Control:

The state imposes its technical control over companies by imposing special obligations that they must submit to, and these obligations are represented in the formation of technical reserves and balances, technical debts, as well as other obligations.

State control through the imposition of other obligations:

The insurance company must also send to the Control Department before July 31 of each year the annual proceeds, the report of its activity, the table of accounts, statistics and all the necessary documents associated with it, whose list and forms are determined by a decision of the Minister in charge of Finance, and it must also publish the annual results and the results accounts in at least two national journals, one of which shall be in Arabic, in addition to its commitment to bookkeeping and records.
